üåê The Four Basic Layers of Blockchain:

1. Application/Services layer:

Here is where the user expresses. It may be viewed as the interface that connects users to the functionalities of blockchain; therefore, it includes applications such as decentralized finance (DeFi) and non-fungible tokens (NFTs).

2. Data layer:

This is where data is stored and immutable, transparent, and secure‚Äîthat is where the heart of blockchain rests. The use of data structures, such as Merkle trees, enables efficient verification of data and assurance of its integrity across the blocks.

3. Consensus layer:

How do we reach a consensus on what is true on any decentralized platform? Included in this layer are mechanisms such as PoW, PoS, and DPoS. Each comes with its strengths:

- PoW: Uses a lot of energy but is very secure.

- PoS: Energy-efficient and scalable.

- DPoS translates into fast transactions through delegated validators.

4. Network layer:

The communication where peer-to-peer connections in the blockchain ecosystem happen; allows connectivity and data exchange among nodes, thereby creating a powerful network.
